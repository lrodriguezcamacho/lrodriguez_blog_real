Introduction

The gender pay gap is a global issue, with women consistently earning less than men. Despite significant progress made by many countries, this continues to be a persistent problem. In this project, we will examine how the United States has addressed the gender pay gap over the period from 1988 to 2023. Additionally, we will explore how the pay gap varies across different racial and ethnic groups. This will help us answer the question: Do men consistently earn more than women within each racial/ethnic group, or are there any exceptions?

Prediction

Based on personal knowledge, I assume that men earn more than women across all racial and ethnic groups, regardless of background.

Data Source

The data used in this project was obtained from a dataset on Kaggle, originally sourced from the U.S. Department of Labor.

Approach

The data set provides information on median annual salaries in the United States, broken down by race and sex, from 1988 to 2023. According to the data set owner on Kaggle, the data was downloaded from the official website of the U.S. Department of Labor. Early years that did not include salary data for certain ethnic groups were removed. The data set was then reorganized to improve accessibility and analytical usability.

The variables in the data set include:

  • Year

  • Salary

  • Sex (Male and Female)

  • Race/Ethnicity (Asian, White, Black, and Hispanic)

Tools

The tools used for this project include:

  • R and RStudio on Posit Cloud

  • GitHub for website hosting

  • The tidyverse package for data analysis and visualization

Approach/Predictions

To answer the question of whether men consistently earn more than women within each racial/ethnic group, and to identify any exceptions, we will create a series of visualizations. These graphs will help us compare salaries within each racial/ethnic and gender group and reveal how gender and race/ethnicity correlate with income levels. Additionally, we will produce a graph comparing the earnings of white women to those of non-white men.

Based on this analysis, my prediction is that white men will earn more than individuals in any other racial/ethnic or gender group.
